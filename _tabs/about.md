I am a neuroethology researcher interested in how animals process sensory information to make decisions in complex environments.

My current research focuses on the neural and behavioral mechanisms underlying collective-motion-related decision making in desert locusts, with particular emphasis on visual processing, attention, and collective behavior.

Using body and head kinematic monitoring, carefully controlled visual stimuli, and neurophysiological recordings, I investigate how sensory information is transformed into adaptive behavior. I also have experience working with rodent model systems.
